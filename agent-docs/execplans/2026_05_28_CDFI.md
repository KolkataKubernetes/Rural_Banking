## Surprises & Discoveries

- Observation: The original combined plan mixed two different kinds of work: data-contract decisions and descriptive notebook design.
  Evidence: the original draft had to specify raw-file harmonization, sample rules, and figure-selection rules in one document, which made the notebook plan too dependent on unresolved ingest choices.

- Observation: The staged memo `agent-docs/agent_context/docs/draft_es_cdfi.docx` is useful as a figure blueprint but should not control raw-data design decisions.
  Evidence: it describes time-series totals, county maps, per-capita views, and peer-state comparisons, but it is prose about outputs rather than a cleaned-data contract.

- Observation: The notebook can be cleaner and more reproducible if it reads one harmonized intermediate rather than directly ingesting every yearly raw release.
  Evidence: the staged CDFI releases span multiple schemas and one distinct NMTC workbook, which would make a standalone notebook harder to audit if it had to solve those ingest issues inline.

- Observation: The county story is materially more stable in a pooled `2018-2020` window than in a literal latest-year snapshot.
  Evidence: the ingest coverage audit shows strong date-based assignment through `2020`, while `2021-2022` rely heavily on report-year fallback and produce noisier county-level interpretation.

- Observation: An exploratory industry composition figure is feasible, but only as a pooled candidate rather than a late-tail annual series.
  Evidence: usable industry coverage is high in `2018-2020` and then drops sharply in `2021-2022`, making a recent annual sector figure harder to defend.

- Observation: Quarto on this machine does not default to the same working R used elsewhere in the repo.
  Evidence: a plain `quarto render` call picked up a broken Anaconda R, while `QUARTO_R=/usr/local/bin/Rscript quarto render ...` completed successfully.

## Candidate Figure Inventory

The notebook should aim to render 8-10 candidate visuals before recommending the strongest 3-5 for the final report.

1. Wisconsin annual CDFI lending volume in dollars.
2. Wisconsin annual CDFI loan count.
3. Wisconsin annual average CDFI loan size.
4. Wisconsin annual count of unique reporting CDFI organizations active in Wisconsin.
5. Wisconsin county map of total CDFI lending in the latest comparable year.
6. Wisconsin county map of CDFI loan count in the latest comparable year.
7. Wisconsin county map or ranked lollipop of CDFI lending per resident in the latest comparable year or a cumulative window supported by the denominator data.
8. Wisconsin versus peer states time series for CDFI loan count per million residents.
9. Wisconsin versus peer states time series for CDFI lending dollars per million residents.
10. Wisconsin versus `Midwest (excl. WI)`, `National (excl. CA, MA, NY)`, and `National` on an annual index or average-state basis.
11. Wisconsin sector composition view if the ingest audit shows industry completeness is strong enough.
12. Wisconsin purpose or targeted-population composition view if the ingest audit shows those fields are interpretable.

The notebook should not force all twelve ideas. It should use the ingest plan’s coverage audit to decide which ideas are strong enough to keep.

## Plan of Work

Implementation should proceed in three milestones.

### Milestone 1: Scaffold the standalone notebook

Create `1_code/workbooks/2026_05_28_cdfi.qmd` with embedded-resource HTML output. Keep helper functions local to the notebook. Reuse the same overall structure seen in the existing workbooks: title block, motivation, setup chunk, figure sections, and a closing recommendation section.

After the Wisconsin-only section, add peer-state and state-group comparisons using the same benchmark definitions already present elsewhere in the repo. End with a concise recommendation section that identifies the best 3-5 figures and a short “not recommended now” list for candidates dropped because of data weakness or redundancy.
